Loaning

Since the abolition of the 'Basis beurs', studying in the Netherlands has become more expensive than ever. Therefore, it is not strange to see that many students are loaning money from the government in order to pay for their books, rent and hobbies. However, without proper research, many students face a surprise when they have a debt of â‚¬30.000,- as soon as they are graduated. Before you start loaning money, read our tips regarding student loans. This will save you troubles and money!

Income Parents

First of all, it is wise to check whether you actually need to loan for your studies. Sit down with your parents and ask if they are able to support you financially and if yes, how much can they give you per month? Is this amount enough to pay for everything you need? Also, it is important to ask how much your parents earn per year. If they earn below a certain amount, you might receive some money from the government after all!

Calculate!

Now that you know how much you will get from you parents and perhaps the government, it is time to calculate how much you should probably loan. Take the general student expenses from NIBUD we talked about in General Study Costs and costumize them untill they fit your needs. Then, substract your incoming money from the expenses and the remainder is the gap in your budget you could fill with a loan!

The loan

Before you immediately loan the maximum amount of money, it is important to know the terms and conditions of this student loan. Currently, the interest is 0%, which is really favorable. This means that you only have to pay back what you loaned, without any extra costs. As soon as you graduate, you have 30 years to repay the money to the government.

Warnings

You should be aware that throughout the years, the interest rate can change and the costs may become higher than expected. Also, you are obliged to finish a study within 10 years. This starts from the moment you start loaning money. If you have no degree after these years, you have to pay your entire loaning (and OV-chip card costs) back. Loaning money costs money!

Student jobs

For the independent ones, there is another possible source of income, besides your parents and the government. Since nobody wants to work long hours at the supermarket with a minimum salary, we have a list of jobs that best fit a student's needs.

  • Student assistent

    A job at the university often offers a pretty great salary! You can help first-year students with their problems regarding the study, work on open days, inform high-school children about the university or your study, etc. The options are endless! Also: it looks excellent on your resume!

  • Homework assistent

    Either universities or independent companies offer high-school students assistence with their homework and courses. You already have the knowledge - you didn't graduate from high-school for no reason -, so helping some children with their maths or languages should not be a problem! And, of course, it often pays quite well!

  • Chauffeur

    As a driver, you simply transport cars to a different destination. With public transport, you pick up the car and then you drive to wherever the car needs to be for e.g. a client of the company. Great salary included!

Working hours

As a student, your schedule will most likely differ every day. Therefore, it is wise to find a job that offers flexible working hours. In the ideal situation, you can decide for each specific week when you are available. This way, you don't have to worry about working during exam weeks!

Eyes on the future

When looking for a part-time job, it is a great idea to think about how that job would appear on your resume. See whether you can find something in the field of your studies or where you can learn valuable skills for future jobs. Thinking ahead is key!

Saving Money

Expert tips!

  • Try to save 10% of your total income!

  • Create different saving accounts for different saving goals!

  • Set an automatic transfer to your saving account(s) each month for a certain amount you want to save

  • Make a budget plan with your incomes and expenses to get an overview of how much you can save per month

  • Don't use money from your saving account(s) for other expenses, if it is not an emergency
